# Olive Branch Network DAO Light Paper

## Protocol Fund Decision Concept

### 1. Overview

The Olive Branch Network is designed to align individual participation with collective impact. Through staking, users are able to earn OBN while a portion of protocol emissions is directed toward nonprofits and long-term ecosystem development.

As OBN moves toward decentralized governance, the protocol’s `charityFund` and treasury wallet addresses are intended to evolve from standard wallet addresses into dedicated smart contracts. These contracts will accumulate OBN over time and allow the community to participate in deciding how those funds are used.

This governance process will be known as the **Protocol Fund Decision**.

The Protocol Fund Decision is an annual DAO vote that gives OBN participants the ability to help determine the future use of accumulated protocol funds.

---

### 2. Purpose of the Protocol Fund Decision

The purpose of the Protocol Fund Decision is to create a transparent, predictable, and community-driven process for handling OBN accumulated by the protocol.

Instead of allowing the `charityFund` and treasury balances to remain controlled by traditional wallets, the protocol will move these balances into smart contracts with clear governance rules. After each one-year accumulation period, eligible users will be able to vote on what happens to the OBN held inside those contracts.

This creates a recurring governance cycle where the community helps decide whether accumulated OBN should be burned, redirected, or distributed to nonprofits on the platform.

---

### 3. Smart Contract Fund Structure

The protocol will eventually transition the following wallet addresses into smart contract-based fund structures:

#### Treasury Fund Contract

The treasury contract will accumulate the OBN allocated to the protocol treasury.

The treasury exists to support the long-term health, sustainability, and strategic direction of the Olive Branch Network. However, under the Protocol Fund Decision model, the accumulated treasury balance will not automatically be used by the team or protocol. Instead, after a one-year accumulation period, users will vote on what happens to the OBN held in the treasury contract.

#### Charity Fund Contract

The `charityFund` contract will accumulate the OBN allocated to the protocol’s charity fund.

The `charityFund` exists to increase the impact of the Olive Branch Network by directing additional OBN toward nonprofits on the platform. After a one-year accumulation period, users will vote on which nonprofit receives the accumulated `charityFund` balance.

---

### 4. Annual Accumulation Period

Each fund contract will accumulate OBN for a period of one year.

During this period, the contracts will receive OBN according to the protocol’s existing emission and allocation structure. The funds will remain locked inside the smart contracts until the annual Protocol Fund Decision vote occurs.

This one-year cycle is designed to reduce governance fatigue, avoid constant voting, and give each decision enough significance to matter.

Rather than requiring users to vote on every small movement of funds, the protocol creates one major annual decision point where the community determines the direction of accumulated protocol value.

---

### 5. Protocol Fund Decision Vote Order

The Protocol Fund Decision will follow a specific order.

The treasury vote will happen first. The `charityFund` vote will happen second.

This order matters because the treasury vote may affect the final amount of OBN held by the `charityFund`.

---

### 6. Step One: Treasury Fund Decision

The first vote in the Protocol Fund Decision will determine what happens to the OBN accumulated in the treasury contract.

Users will vote between two options:

1. **Burn the accumulated treasury OBN**
2. **Transfer the accumulated treasury OBN to the `charityFund` contract**

If users vote to burn the treasury balance, the accumulated OBN in the treasury contract will be permanently removed from circulation.

If users vote to transfer the treasury balance to the `charityFund`, the accumulated OBN will be moved into the `charityFund` contract and added to the amount that will later be directed toward a nonprofit.

This gives the community a choice between reducing supply and increasing charitable impact.

The treasury decision represents one of the most important philosophical choices within the protocol: whether accumulated treasury value should strengthen token scarcity through burning or strengthen direct public-good impact through nonprofit funding.

---

### 7. Step Two: Charity Fund Decision

After the treasury vote is completed, the second vote will determine which nonprofit on the Olive Branch Network platform receives the accumulated `charityFund` balance.

Users will vote for one eligible nonprofit from the platform.

The winning nonprofit will receive the OBN accumulated in the `charityFund` contract. If the treasury vote results in the treasury balance being transferred to the `charityFund`, then the selected nonprofit may receive both the accumulated `charityFund` balance and the transferred treasury amount.

This allows the community to collectively decide which organization should receive additional support from the protocol.

The `charityFund` vote turns OBN governance into a recurring act of public-good decision-making. Instead of charity being controlled only by the founding team, the community itself helps decide where protocol-generated support should go.

---

### 8. Governance Philosophy

The Protocol Fund Decision is built around three core principles:

#### Transparency

Funds should not move silently or unpredictably. By placing the `charityFund` and treasury into smart contracts, users can see how much OBN has accumulated and understand when the next vote will occur.

#### Community Direction

The Olive Branch Network is not only a staking protocol. It is a coordination system for people who want value to flow toward meaningful causes. The Protocol Fund Decision allows users to help guide that flow.

#### Long-Term Alignment

Annual voting creates a rhythm for governance. It gives the protocol time to accumulate meaningful balances while giving the community a recurring opportunity to make important decisions.

---

### 9. Why the Treasury Vote Comes First

The treasury vote comes before the `charityFund` vote because the treasury decision can change the final `charityFund` balance.

If the community votes to burn the treasury OBN, the `charityFund` vote will only apply to the OBN accumulated directly inside the `charityFund` contract.

If the community votes to transfer the treasury OBN to the `charityFund`, the `charityFund` vote will apply to a larger amount.

This creates a clear and logical process:

1. First, the community decides whether treasury OBN should be burned or added to charitable impact.
2. Second, the community decides which nonprofit should receive the `charityFund` balance.

---

### 10. Potential Benefits

The Protocol Fund Decision may create several benefits for the Olive Branch Network.

It gives OBN holders a meaningful reason to participate in governance. It increases transparency around protocol-controlled funds. It turns the `charityFund` into a recurring community-directed impact mechanism. It gives the treasury a clear and accountable purpose. It also introduces a simple governance model that is easy for users to understand.

Most importantly, it reinforces the mission of OBN: to create a system where value does not only reward participation, but also supports organizations doing good in the world.

---

### 11. Conceptual Example

Assume the treasury contract and `charityFund` contract each accumulate OBN for one year.

At the end of the year, the Protocol Fund Decision begins.

First, users vote on the treasury balance. If the treasury contract has accumulated OBN, users choose whether that OBN should be burned or transferred to the `charityFund`.

If the vote favors burning, the treasury OBN is removed from circulation.

If the vote favors transferring, the treasury OBN is moved into the `charityFund` contract.

Next, users vote on which nonprofit receives the `charityFund` balance. The nonprofit with the winning vote receives the accumulated OBN from the `charityFund` contract.

This process repeats each year.

---

### 12. Long-Term Vision

The Protocol Fund Decision is an early step toward DAO governance for the Olive Branch Network.

It does not require every protocol decision to be decentralized immediately. Instead, it introduces a focused and meaningful governance process around two important areas: treasury direction and charitable distribution.

Over time, this model can expand as the protocol matures. Future governance may include nonprofit onboarding, nonprofit offboarding, emission schedule decisions, protocol upgrades, ecosystem grants, and other major decisions.

The Protocol Fund Decision gives the DAO a clear starting point.

It allows the community to practice governance through decisions that are simple, transparent, and directly tied to the purpose of the protocol.

---

### 13. Conclusion

The Olive Branch Network was created to connect staking, rewards, and real-world impact.

By turning the `charityFund` and treasury wallet addresses into smart contracts, the protocol can move toward a more transparent and community-directed future.

Through the annual Protocol Fund Decision, users will help determine whether accumulated treasury OBN should be burned or transferred to the `charityFund`. After that, users will vote on which nonprofit receives the `charityFund` balance.

This creates a recurring cycle of accumulation, participation, and impact.

The Protocol Fund Decision is not just a governance mechanism. It is a reflection of what OBN is trying to become: a network where value is guided by the people who believe it should be used to uplift the world.
